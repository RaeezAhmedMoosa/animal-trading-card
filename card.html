Great White Shark

In 1991, South Africa became the first country in the world to legally protect great white sharks.

  • Scientific Name: Carcharodon Carcharias
  • Average Length: 4.7 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 70 years
  • Habitat: Coastal surface waters

Great white sharks are large sturdy fishes with a torpedo-like body shape. Their backs and sides have a contrasting pattern of dark blue, gray or brown with a stark white underbelly. They have a maximum length of around 6.4 meters and have a mass between 680 and 1800 kilograms.
